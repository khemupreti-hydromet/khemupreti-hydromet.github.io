This project focuses on the monitoring and risk assessment of the **Lower Barun Glacier Lake**, one of the most significant and potentially dangerous glacial lakes in the eastern Himalayas of Nepal.

### Project Overview
Using multi-temporal satellite imagery. Monitoring these changes is essential for identifying expansion trends that could indicate an increased risk of a Glacial Lake Outburst Flood (GLOF).

![Lower Barun Monitoring](/images/Lower_Barun_Final_V3.gif)
*Animation showing the area changes of the Lower Barun Glacier Lake.*

### Technical Focus:
* **Manual Delineation:** High-precision mapping to separate lake water from surrounding moraine and debris-covered ice.
* **Tools:** Python,GEE and MNDWI.

### Significance
As climate change accelerates glacier retreat, monitoring these lakes is a priority for downstream community safety. This work provides a verifiable record of lake dynamics, contributing to cryosphere research in the Himalayan region.
